HOME MADE HALLOWEEN DECORATIONS

Halloween has become one of the most favored holidays of the year. Between dressing up in costumes, going to parties and the beloved tradition of trick-or-treat there isn't much to love about it. Below are some DIY decoration ideas and how to make them.

Another favorite part of Halloween is the decorations. Almost everyone puts up some sort of interior decorations but lawn decorations are getting more and more popular over the years. Now, one way to go about this of course is to purchase outdoor lawn decorations but the cost can add up quickly and they don't always last very long in the unkind October weather. One way to get around this is to build/create your own lawn decorations. For almost half the cost, if not more, you can make your own decorations out of inexpensive material that will last against the weather, and as you will see it is not hard at all.

Lawn Decoration Ideas

When it comes to Halloween lawn decorations there are many different routes you can take. One of the most popular is the cemetary. This can be as simple as a few home made tomb stones with custom engravings or you can get even more elaborate by adding a rickety fence. Other popular decorations are ghosts, witches, and spider webs

Cemetery

DIY cemeteries are very easy and inexpensive to make at home. I have found that the best material to use is 1.5 inch or 2 inch insulation styrofoam from either Lowes or HomeDepot. One 4' x 8' sheet can allow you to make at least four tombstones depending on what size you make them.

Start by lightly drawing the form of your tomb stones onto the styrofoam. Then proceed by cutting out the shapes with a small hand saw. Once your tombstones are cut out. Use your creativity to cut out any "engravings" you would like on each one. This step can be done by using a small exacto knife and chipping away at styrofoam. You can also print out a template to trace over with the exacto knife and then cut them out.

Your next step will be to spray paint your tombstones with a gray or light gray color. Once that dries add black paint to your engravings to make them stand out.

Once all of your paint has dried, the final step is to push two skewers into the very bottom of the tombstone. Two predrilled holes help make this step easier. Just make sure you don't drill holes much larger then the thickness of your skewers.

Ghosts

Depending on how in-depth you would like to get, ghosts can be done very easily or with a little bit more difficulty. Eiether way is significantly less money then purchasing them at a store.

Easy Lighted Hanging Ghosts

    Items Needed
  • Dollar Tree paper lanterns (Amazon also sells them)
  • Dollar Tree round plastic table covers (also on Amazon)
  • Packing tape (Dollar Tree has the Jot brand)
  • Scissors
  • String or twine
  • Black adhesive vinyl (Dollar Tree now carries adhesive vinyl, but you could also use a black garbage bag or color in the eyes and mouth with a Sharpie)
  • Double-sided tape (if using a black garbage bag for eyes and mouth)
  • Paper lantern lights (optional)
  • Garden shepherdâ€™s hooks or a tree with branches you can reach (or you can hang them indoors)

    • First step will be to assemble the paper latterns. Then hang the latern lights (if you purchased them) inside the paper latern by tieing them to the center wire support. You will next open the white table covers and unfold it until you find the center. Once you found the center, get a few pieces of packing tape and drape them over. (You will have to either fold the table cover back in half if it's opened all the way, just make sure you have a crease in it before taping the center). Adding the tape will help in reinforce it when the wind blows.

    Before fully opening the table cover, cut a tiny hole at the center, where you taped it. Then fully open the table cover.

    Now pull the lattern hook up and through the hole you cut in the table cover. Letting the table cover loosely drape down and over the lattern.

    The next step is to tie string to the lattern hook, you pulled threw the hole in the table cover. You can finish up here by tieing off the other end of the string to a tree branch or hanging hook or add eyes you cut out of the black adhesive tape.
